This is the correct direction. For forensic-grade blockchain intelligence, every scenario should be modeled as an **Asset Lifecycle Pattern (ALP)** rather than a single transaction. The investigation objective is to preserve **asset continuity** across transformations and identify the eventual custodial endpoints, while acknowledging that some flows (especially through privacy systems) can only be linked probabilistically rather than with certainty.

A robust investigation framework for each scenario should include:

* Asset lifecycle
* Transaction sequence
* Detection indicators
* Intelligence to extract
* Data to parse
* Evidence artifacts
* Continuity scoring
* Graph orchestration
* Algorithms
* Trace continuation logic
* Expected outputs

# Final Investigation Outputs

For every traced seed wallet, generate:

* **Asset Recovery Matrix:** original asset, transformed asset(s), current asset(s), and remaining traceable value.
* **Wallet Landing Matrix:** every identified recipient wallet, grouped by blockchain, entity, and confidence.
* **Exchange & Custodian Exposure:** detected deposits into known exchanges, custodians, payment processors, brokers, and institutional wallets, with estimated deposited amounts.
* **Asset Lineage Graph:** end-to-end provenance showing every transformation, bridge, swap, and custody transition.
* **Traceability Assessment:** percentage of funds deterministically linked versus probabilistically inferred, with explicit confidence scores and evidence supporting each conclusion. This provides a defensible forensic record while avoiding overstating certainty where the on-chain evidence does not support it.
