Phase 1: Incident Response

Immediately executed upon discovery of the theft or unauthorized transfer. The critical window for asset recovery is the first 48 hours.

Objectives

  • Preserve digital evidence in its original state (RAM capture if device is live).
  • Prevent further losses (revoke smart contract approvals, isolate infected devices).
  • Begin strict chain of custody documentation.

Detailed Actions

Upon notification of an incident, the immediate priority is triage. The victim's remaining assets must be secured. This involves revoking malicious token allowances using tools like Revoke.cash or migrating remaining funds to an entirely fresh, uncompromised hardware wallet. Simultaneously, investigators will conduct a preliminary interview to establish the timeline of events and potential vectors of compromise (e.g., phishing links, downloaded malware, compromised seed phrase storage).

LIONSGATE

Phase 2: Evidence Preservation

The systematic capture of volatile digital evidence to ensure admissibility in future legal proceedings under Daubert standards.

Objectives

  • Capture web browser artifacts, extensions (e.g., MetaMask logs), and device memory.
  • Document communication with malicious actors (phishing emails, Telegram logs).
  • Cryptographically hash (SHA-256) all collected files for integrity verification.

Detailed Actions

Forensic imaging of affected devices (mobile, desktop, cold storage) is conducted if physical compromise is suspected. Phishing domains, malicious smart contracts, and social engineering communications are archived and hashed. The integrity of this data is maintained through strict chain-of-custody logs, ensuring that the evidence is not altered and can withstand cross-examination in civil or criminal court.

INTELLIGENCE

Phase 4: Intelligence Collection (OSINT)

Fusing raw blockchain addresses with real-world intelligence to de-anonymize the threat actor.

Key Actions

  • Scraping darknet forums and ransomware leak sites for address mentions.
  • Analyzing GitHub, Telegram, and Discord for developer slip-ups.
  • Deploying NEMESIS Entity Resolution to cross-reference known attacker profiles.

Detailed Actions

A blockchain address alone is merely pseudonymous. To unmask the operator, we scour the open and dark web for instances where the attacker linked their cryptographic identity to a real-world persona. This includes indexing historical forum posts where the address was used for payment, tracking social media handles tied to associated domains, and utilizing proprietary databases to identify known cybercriminal syndicates or advanced persistent threat (APT) groups.

ATTRIBUTION

Phase 5: Entity Attribution

The process of formally linking the cryptographic movement of funds to a distinct, actionable real-world entity or service provider.

Key Actions

  • Identifying the terminal Centralized Exchange (CEX) where funds were deposited (e.g., Binance, Kraken).
  • Determining the jurisdictional location of the holding entity.
  • Drafting the Entity Attribution Assessment report.

Detailed Actions

The ultimate goal of blockchain forensics is to trace the funds to a regulated entity, known as a Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) or Centralized Exchange (CEX). Once funds hit a CEX, the blockchain trail goes cold, but the legal trail gets hot. The CEX holds the user's Know-Your-Customer (KYC) identity, bank account details, and IP addresses. We definitively attribute the destination wallet to a specific CEX using heuristics, test transactions, and public database correlations.

LEGAL

Phase 6: Legal Case Building

Translating technical forensic findings into legally actionable documents for attorneys and judges.

Key Actions

  • Drafting Expert Witness Declarations explaining the blockchain tracing.
  • Preparing the civil complaint or law enforcement referral packet.
  • Compiling all exhibits into a court-ready master file.

Detailed Actions

Attorneys and judges are rarely blockchain experts. Our team bridges this gap by writing clear, sworn declarations that explain complex topics like "peel chains," "mixers," and "cross-chain bridges" in plain English. We structure the forensic findings so that legal counsel can seamlessly integrate them into their motions for injunctive relief or discovery.

REPORTING

Phase 7: Reporting

The formal delivery of the Master Forensic Trace Report to the client and legal counsel.

Key Actions

  • Final review of tracing accuracy and confidence scores.
  • Delivery of interactive graphs and raw CSV ledgers.
  • Briefing the legal team on the technical mechanics of the theft.

Detailed Actions

The final Master Forensic Trace Report is compiled, containing high-resolution flowcharts, annotated transaction hashes, and clear conclusions regarding the terminus of the funds. This document is the cornerstone of the recovery effort, designed to be handed directly to law enforcement agencies or filed as an exhibit in civil court.

ACTION

Phase 8: Exchange Notification

Serving Preservation Requests to the identified Centralized Exchanges to prevent the dissipation of funds.

Key Actions

  • Issuing formal legal hold requests to the exchange's legal department.
  • Providing the exchange with the exact deposit hashes and timestamps.
  • Requesting preservation of KYC/AML records and login IPs.

Detailed Actions

Before a court order can be obtained, the exchange must be placed on notice. We issue urgent preservation letters to the compliance departments of the identified VASPs. This letter details the illicit nature of the deposited funds and demands that the exchange freeze the account and preserve all associated data under threat of secondary liability or spoliation of evidence claims.

LITIGATION

Phase 9: Civil Litigation

Initiating formal legal proceedings against "John Doe" defendants or identified actors in civil court to recover damages.

Key Actions

  • Filing the civil complaint.
  • Filing motions for alternative service (e.g., serving via NFT drop to the attacker's wallet).
  • Seeking damages for conversion and unjust enrichment.

Detailed Actions

If law enforcement is unavailable or too slow, civil litigation is the primary vehicle for recovery. Attorneys file a "John Doe" lawsuit against the unknown attacker. Given the pseudonymous nature of crypto, we assist counsel in obtaining court permission for "alternative service of process"—such as sending a legal summons embedded in an NFT directly to the hacker's wallet address.

ENFORCEMENT

Phase 10: Criminal Investigation

Escalating the case file to federal authorities (FBI, Secret Service, DOJ) for parallel criminal proceedings.

Key Actions

  • Filing comprehensive complaints with the IC3.
  • Direct liaison with Cyber Task Force agents.
  • Providing structured data feeds to law enforcement databases.

Detailed Actions

A civil suit and criminal investigation often run in parallel. We package the forensic findings into a format optimized for federal law enforcement intake. By doing the heavy lifting of tracing and attribution, we significantly increase the likelihood that an agency will adopt the case, issue criminal subpoenas, and utilize mutual legal assistance treaties (MLATs) for international exchanges.

JUDICIAL

Phase 11: Court Orders

Obtaining the necessary judicial decrees to unmask the attacker and compel the exchange to seize the assets.

Key Actions

  • Obtaining Norwich Pharmacal or Subpoena Duces Tecum orders for KYC data.
  • Securing Mareva Injunctions or Temporary Restraining Orders (TROs) to formally freeze funds.
  • Serving the orders on the centralized exchanges.

Detailed Actions

Using the forensic report and expert declarations as evidence, counsel petitions the court for discovery orders. Once granted, these orders compel the exchange to reveal the true identity of the account holder. Concurrently, injunctions are served to transform the temporary preservation hold into a legally binding freeze, preventing the exchange from releasing the funds.

RESTITUTION

Phase 12: Asset Restitution

The final legal and technical maneuvers required to return the frozen funds to the rightful owner.

Key Actions

  • Obtaining a final judgment or settlement agreement.
  • Executing turnover orders against the exchange.
  • Securely repatriating the assets to a clean, cold-storage wallet.

Detailed Actions

Once identity is established and liability proven (or a default judgment obtained), the court issues a final order directing the exchange to liquidate or transfer the frozen assets back to the victim. We assist in establishing secure, audited cold-storage environments to receive the restituted funds, ensuring the recovery process concludes safely.
